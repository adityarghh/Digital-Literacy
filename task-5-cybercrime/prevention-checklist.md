# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist

*For college students in India | CSE0001 – Digital Literacy*

---

## General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across platforms.
- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts: email, social media, and banking apps.
- [ ] **3. Keep your phone's OS and apps updated** — security patches close vulnerabilities that fraudsters actively exploit.
- [ ] **4. Download apps only from official sources** — Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Never install APKs from unknown websites.
- [ ] **5. Never click links in SMS or WhatsApp** claiming to be from banks, NPCI, government agencies, or KYC update services.
- [ ] **6. Lock your phone with a PIN or biometric** — if your phone is lost or stolen, this is your first line of defence.

---

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety *(Required)*

- [ ] **7. Never enter your UPI PIN to "receive" money** — entering a PIN always authorises a payment OUT of your account, never into it. There is no PIN required to receive.
- [ ] **8. Verify all UPI collect requests before approving** — if you do not recognise the requester, reject it immediately. Legitimate buyers do not need your PIN.

---

## Social & Identity Safety

- [ ] **9. Never share OTPs, CVV numbers, or full card details** with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank or government.
- [ ] **10. Do not overshare personal information online** — your full address, phone number, and ID details should never appear on public social media profiles.

---

## 🚨 Reporting Channels

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — file online complaints for all cybercrimes |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — 24x7 toll-free helpline; call immediately for financial fraud to maximise recovery chances |

> **Tip:** The faster you report financial fraud to your bank AND call 1930, the higher the chance of recovering your money. Do not wait.
